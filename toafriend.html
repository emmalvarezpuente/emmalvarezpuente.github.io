To a Friend

If you come to see me, for instead, al four in the afternoon, from tree in the afternoon I will begin to be happy. As the time approaches the hour I will feel but happy.  And At  four I will be shaken and I will worry myself, I will discover the price of the happiness!  But if you come at any time I will never know at what time to dress my heart...  The rites are necessary.

What is a rite?  it said the Little Prince.     Is what causes that one day is different from other days.  One hour of the other hours...  It is part of the conversation that maintains the fox with the Little Prince, one of my favourite stories.

  I will emphasize by its sensitivity, the dedicatory of Antoine de Saint-Exupery to its friend Leon Werth.

 I ask pardon to the children by dedicate this book to a greater person. 

I have a serious excuse:

 This greater person is the best friend than I ever have in the world.

  I have another excuse:

  This greater person is able to understand everything, even books for children. 

I have a third excuse: 

 This greater person lives in France where suffer  hunger and cold.  Truly, he needs to be consoled. 

If all these excuses were not enough, I can dedicate this book to the child who once was this greater person all the greater ones have been first children, but few remember it.  I correct then my dedicatory:

 "To Leon Werth when he was a child."
